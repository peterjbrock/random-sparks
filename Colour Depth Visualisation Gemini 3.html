Key Teaching Concepts

  • Additive Mixing: Selecting multiple channels shows how combined step boundaries form compound colors (e.g., Red + Green forms Yellow steps).
  • Grayscale Baseline: Selecting all three channels yields true grayscale, showing how raw brightness data splits.
  • Quantization: Moving down the stack shows continuous analog real-world information forced into discrete numeric blocks.
  • The Math: Each added bit doubles the structural gray/color steps (2n).
  • Human Factor: Explain why 8-bit (256 variations) serves as standard media baseline because our eyes struggle to spot boundaries at that resolution.
